<p>Amazon has this nice service aptly named <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/browse.html?node=16427261">Simple Storage Service</a> (abbreviated S3), where they aim to provide simple storage of <i>data</i> of arbitrary size, with high availability at consumer-level cost. It really is a nice <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amazon_S3#Design">design</a>. And there's a REST interface setup for me to store my data!</p>
<p>And <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jeremy_Zawodny">Jeremy Zawodny</a> points out how using this service actually ends up to be cheaper (and more reliable) than having a home media server!</p>
<blockquote><p>That puts the total cost for 5 years worth of backups [on a home backup server] around $2,275 assuming that no hardware breaks.</p>
<p>Switching to S3 could save me $587 over five years!</p>
